Harley Davidson Motorcycles

Touring CVO Softail
Ultra Limited CVO Street Glide Deluxe

The Touring motorcycles are known as "dressers" or "baggers" that uses the Big-Twin engines. The CVO (Custom Vehicle Operations) motorcycles is a limited-edition customizations of Harley’s stock models with added higher-displacement engines, performance upgrades and special-edition paint jobs. The Softail models are similar to the hardtail choppers which was popular in the 60’s and 70’s keeping the tradition that also uses the big-twin engines.

The nickname "HOG"

A bunch of farm boys who consistently won races in the 1920’s became known as the "hog boys". They had a hog as their mascot and would put the hog on their Harley after winning a race and take what they call a victory lap. In 1983, Harley Davidson took the name “HOG” and turned that name into an acronym naming it Harley Owners Group.
